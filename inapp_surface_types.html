In-app surface types

What each one does, when to reach for it, and why — plus how they work together. Most lifecycle moments call for more than one surface; the choice signals to the user how much attention the moment requires.

Use cases Onboarding Feature adoption Conversion Retention Support deflection Feedback Expansion
Surface 01
Product tours & flows
🗺️

Step-by-step walkthroughs that guide users through a task inside the product. Each step highlights a UI element and prompts an action before advancing. The most interactive surface — the user is doing, not just reading.

Use cases
Onboarding Feature adoption Support deflection
Good for
  • First-time workflows with multiple steps where someone could get lost
  • Replacing a support ticket — "show me how to do X" becomes a self-serve walkthrough
  • Introducing a new feature that isn't self-explanatory
  • Re-engaging dormant users on a workflow they've never completed
Example triggers
user.first_login = true feature_X_visited AND flow_not_completed support_topic = "how do I..."
Not ideal when: the user already knows the workflow. A tour shown to an experienced user feels patronizing and trains them to dismiss.
Surface 02
Checklists
✅

A persistent panel showing onboarding or activation progress as a list of tasks. Items check off as users complete them. Lower friction than a tour — the user moves at their own pace rather than being walked step by step.

Use cases
Onboarding Retention Feature adoption
Good for
  • Users who prefer to self-direct rather than be walked through steps
  • Multi-session onboarding where someone needs to pick back up
  • Showing users what "fully set up" looks like so they know when they're done
  • Feature adoption campaigns — "here are 3 things you haven't tried yet"
Example triggers
signup completed onboarding_checklist_pct < 100 days_since_last_login > 7
Not ideal when: the task list exceeds 5–6 items. More than that feels like homework. Also less effective for single-session, high-intent moments where a tour is the right call.
Surface 03
Tooltips
💬

Small contextual callouts attached to a specific UI element. Can be persistent (on hover) or triggered (on first visit or after a launch). Lowest interruption of any surface — the user stays in their workflow entirely.

Use cases
Feature adoption Onboarding Support deflection
Good for
  • Clarifying what a button, field, or setting actually does
  • Pointing to a new feature without interrupting the current task
  • Just-in-time help at a decision point — right before a user clicks something risky
  • Reducing tickets on questions that could be answered in-context
Example triggers
element hover new_feature_released AND user.plan = paid first visit to settings page
Not ideal when: the explanation needs more than two sentences. At that point a tour or resource center article fits better. Dense tooltips get skimmed past.
Surface 04
Announcements & banners
📣

In-product messages that interrupt (modals) or persist (banners) to deliver a message broadly, targeted by segment. The in-app equivalent of a broadcast email — except the user is actively in the product when they see it.

Use cases
Feature adoption Conversion Expansion Retention
Good for
  • New feature launches where you need broad awareness fast
  • Trial expiration warnings ("Your trial ends in 3 days")
  • Plan limit warnings ("You've used 90% of your flow quota")
  • Upsell moments when a user tries to access a paid feature
  • Time-sensitive announcements — downtime, policy changes, events
Example triggers
trial_days_remaining = 3 flow_count / plan_limit > 0.9 feature_launch_date = today
Not ideal when: the message isn't urgent or relevant to what the user is doing. Overused modals train users to dismiss without reading. Frequency and relevance are credibility.
Surface 05
Resource center
📚

A persistent panel (launched from a button in the product UI) giving users on-demand access to guided tours, knowledge base articles, checklists, and AI-assisted help. Users pull from it when they need it — nothing is pushed.

Use cases
Support deflection Onboarding Feature adoption
Good for
  • Reducing inbound support tickets — users find answers themselves
  • Giving users agency to explore at their own pace post-onboarding
  • Surfacing relevant content based on where the user is in the product
  • Housing the checklist and all tours in one persistent place
  • The "I'm stuck" destination that replaces "file a ticket"
Example triggers
always available (persistent) content filtered by current_page FlowAI Actions routes here
Not ideal when: used as a documentation dump. A resource center with 40 articles is as useless as none. Curate to 5–8 highly relevant items per context.
Surface 06
NPS & surveys
📊

In-product survey prompts — NPS score, CSAT, open-ended questions, feature satisfaction polls. Triggered at key lifecycle moments or after specific actions. Higher response rates than email surveys because users are already in context.

Use cases
Feedback Retention Conversion
Good for
  • NPS after a meaningful milestone (first flow published, 30 days paid)
  • Churn intent signals — a declining score is an early warning
  • Feature satisfaction after someone uses something new
  • Qualifying use case at signup to personalize the onboarding path
  • Win/loss signal when a trial user doesn't convert
Example triggers
first_flow_published day_30_of_paid_plan trial_end AND not_converted
Not ideal when: triggered before the user has enough experience to have an opinion, or more than once per quarter. One well-timed survey beats three that get dismissed. Close the loop on responses or the signal is wasted.

Natural pairings for common lifecycle moments
New user activation Checklist (structure) + Tour (walkthrough) + Resource Center (self-serve fallback)
Feature launch Announcement (awareness) + Tooltip (in-context) + Tour (for those who want to go deeper)
